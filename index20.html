Project Overview

Problem Statement

The website for Abbey Cat Adoptions is currently not efficient to use and could use an update. A more professional look and feel would help the organization attract more support and find homes for the cats they have rescued. Specific issues include:

  • Insufficient contrast with grey text on pale yellow background - fails required contrast ratios for accessibility
  • Need for reorganization of website to improve navigation
  • Website is not responsive for mobile
  • Long application form for cat adoption

The Solution:

During interviews and surveys, we discovered that users are interested in supporting local cat adoption organizations by adopting a cat. They need an efficient and easy way to find their ideal potential pet. However, Abbeycats.com currently provides users with an overwhelming amount of information which often can’t be filtered. We believe that users require a more organized and accessible way of finding out about the cats at Abbey Cat Adoptions. We might help if we streamlined the process of finding a cat and improving the application process for adoption. By reorganizing information and making the calls to action on the website, such as adoption or making donations, clear and accessible will not only allow our users to support Abbey Cat Adoptions but also help them adopt a cat faster.

Research

Research methodology consisted of interviews with 5 people, followed by a Google Docs online survey of 33 respondents to learn about how people would approach the cat adoption process. We explained to respondents that we are trying to improve the design of an existing website and we’d like to understand some of the needs and pain points, wants and desires of people who are interested in cat adoption and animal welfare in general.

We also conducted a competitor analysis of three other animal rescue agencies to compare features, strengths and weaknesses of other websites.

In the interviews, we showed respondents the www.abbeycats.org website to get their initial impression of the organization. In the online survey we included a screenshot of the website so respondents wouldn’t need to navigate away from the survey to see the page.

User Persona

User FLow + Storyboard

We reached out to the organization but they were too busy to talk to us so we determine their pain points and needs from their website:

  • Prioritizing requests, high volume of people asking for information
  • Getting volunteers
  • Keeping website up to date with new cats
  • Covering costs of keeping the website up and well managed
  • Covering costs of generally running the organization
  • Managing to keep up with the amount of cats that are given up

They need an efficient way to find volunteers and funding to keep their program running. They need a website that will function to maximize donations, streamline the adoption/fostering process, and provide information to potential clients and volunteers.

Features

  • Donation button higher on the page to make it more prominent
  • 'About Us' info more visible to emphasize charitable status
  • Clearly laid out navigation bar
  • Filter for cats
  • Streamlined application process

Application Redesign

From the original website, the main pain point for the application page is that the content is overwhelming and it could cause their potential customers to give up in this step.

We decided to filter out some unusual questions and reorganized the rest into four separate pages. A progress bar that indecates where the user is really helps users to reduce uncertainty. As well, we added the Micro-interaction design to make the application process stress-free.

Usability Testing

User testing was done to see if the user can access the available cats page and narrow down results using a filter. Next step was for the user to go through the adoption process for a cat by filling out a form and reaching the confirmation page. After the confirmation page, users were asked to return to the home page.

Based on user testing, we broke up the form into single columns on multiple pages, instead of two columns. We modified the progress bar on the multi-page form to improve visibility of system status and let applicants know how far they are in the process. We also added colour to text fields when completed, and a ‘peeking cat micro-animation’ on each page to add delight to an otherwise tedious application process.

We also modified the way the cat’s information appeared on its page, included a photo gallery and a share to social media button.

Summary

Site visitors who are animal lovers looking to adopt a cat may also be potential volunteers or donors to the cause. Increasing engagement through sending newsletters is an audience retention strategy that may strengthen the relationship with the organization and prompt site visitors to provide support.

A quick and easy process for users to make donations, and additional payment options will increase the likelihood of receiving more financial support from the public.

The following features are under consideration to improve the overall user experience:

  • Newsletter/mailing list sign-up button for more engagement with site visitors
  • Receive notifications about new available cats
  • Add FAQs section
  • Make it easier to donate with more options (currently only PayPal/CanadaHelps)
  • Eg. Add E-transfer, Apple Pay
  • Option to save partially completed form and finish later
  • Search function on the website
  • Chatbot for help questions
  • Matchmaker tool to find ideal cat
